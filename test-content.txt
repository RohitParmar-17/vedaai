Chapter: Chemical Effects of Electric Current

Electric current can have various effects when passed through a conducting solution.

1. Electroplating is the process of depositing a layer of metal on another metal using electric current. It is used to prevent corrosion and improve appearance.

2. Electrolysis is the process of decomposition of a substance by passing electric current through its solution. Water can be decomposed into hydrogen and oxygen by electrolysis.

3. A conductor allows electric current to flow through it. Metals like copper, silver, and aluminum are good conductors. Solutions of acids, bases, and salts also conduct electricity.

4. An electrolyte is a substance that conducts electricity in its dissolved or molten state. Examples include sodium chloride, hydrochloric acid, and copper sulfate.

5. Electrodes are the conductors through which current enters or leaves the electrolyte. The positive electrode is called anode and the negative electrode is called cathode.

6. During electrolysis of water, hydrogen gas is collected at cathode and oxygen gas is collected at anode.

7. Copper sulfate solution conducts electricity because it contains free copper ions and sulfate ions.

8. The chemical effect of electric current is used in electroplating, purification of metals, and extraction of metals from their ores.

9. LED (Light Emitting Diode) glows when current passes through it and can be used to test if a solution conducts electricity.

10. Brine (saltwater) when electrolyzed produces chlorine gas at anode, hydrogen gas at cathode, and sodium hydroxide solution.
